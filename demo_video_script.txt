Demo video script, under 2 minutes

0:00-0:10
Open the demo and say: "This demo explains RoPE, or rotary position embeddings. The key idea is that RoPE does not add a position vector. It rotates query and key vectors by position."

0:10-0:25
Go to Step 1. Toggle position encoding. Say: "Self-attention sees token content, but order must be injected separately. The cat chased the dog and The dog chased the cat use similar words, but order changes the meaning."

0:25-0:40
Go to Step 2. Drag the position slider. Say: "In RoPE, position means rotation. As p increases, the query/key vector rotates by p times theta."

0:40-0:58
Go to Step 3. Move m and n, then use Shift both +1 and -1. Say: "When both query and key rotate, their dot product depends on the relative offset. Shifting both tokens keeps the offset the same."

0:58-1:15
Go to Step 4. Drag the position bar. Say: "In real hidden vectors, RoPE groups dimensions into 2D pairs. Each clock is one pair, and each pair has a different theta, so it rotates at a different speed."

1:15-1:32
Go to Step 5. Click different token boxes, then switch to Long periodic. Say: "Here the boxes and curve show toy similarity from the selected query token. The long example makes the periodic structure easier to see."

1:32-1:55
Go to Step 6. Click Play training. Toggle Accuracy and Loss. Say: "Finally, this real tiny training replay compares position methods on an order task: decide whether A appears before B. No position stays near chance, learned absolute fits seen positions less robustly, and RoPE generalizes best in this toy length-16 test."

1:55-2:00
End with: "The takeaway is that RoPE makes position visible inside the attention dot product through rotation."
